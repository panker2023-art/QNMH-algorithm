# Project Context

## Overview
This project aims to develop the **QNMH (Quantitative Network Motif Homology) Algorithm**, a core computational methodology to precisely map the molecular logic of Alzheimer's Disease (AD) pathology between mouse models and humans. It is part of a broader study leveraging a Programmable Genetic Matrix to decode epistatic mechanisms of AD pathogenesis.

## Goals
1. **Epistatic Shielding Analysis**: Identify and validate key epistatic shielding genes in the C57BL/6J genetic background that suppress APOE4 neurotoxicity.
2. **Isomorphism Quantification**: Develop the QNMH algorithm to precisely map and quantify the structural consistency of Gene Regulatory Networks (GRN) between mouse models and human AD brain tissues.
3. **Translational Predictability**: Establish a Precise Risk Index (PRI) to systematically improve the translation efficiency of preclinical drug development.

## Scope
- Construct diverse genetic matrices (hAPOE4-TR × CC strains).
- Analyze multi-omics data (snRNA-seq, Spatial Transcriptomics, Lipidomics).
- Develop and validate the QNMH algorithm based on network motif homology and topological isomorphism.
- Validate causal shielding genes (e.g., *Grn*, *Trem2*) via AAV-CRISPR interventions in B6 mice.
